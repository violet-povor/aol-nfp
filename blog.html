Mindfulness Meditation

December 28, 2018

The Mindfulness Meditation Class with Lou Carcasole was an eye-opening experience. We all learned a great deal about the human mind, such as: it is really hard to think about absolutely nothing, the brain is able to do strange and amazing things etc. Not only that, it also became easier for us to relax and fall asleep.

During each and every single session, even though I wasn't moving at all, I felt like I was flying though the air. Outside of the sessions, my marks in school significantly improved, I was payed more attention in class, and i was slower to anger.

At the very beginning, we were all given a sheet to fill out about things that bothered us(from a scale of 0, not at all, to 4, extremely). We all scored relatively high during the first lesson. However, during the last lesson, we did the sheet again. This timee, it was like binary, we only had 1s and 0s. I was very satisfied with my results.

The Joy of Co-Creativity: The First Lesson!

February 8, 2018

Our first meditative art lesson was very exciting, as mothers were able to do their first art project while connecting with their babies. The information was very interesting and rich as we were learning how our universe is connected to our lives.

The pictures that we drew while listening to a classical composition helped us to understand our inner feelings and create a deeper bonding with ourselves and our babies.

The atmosphere was very warm and welcoming, so we were able to open to each other and share our thoughts and feelings during the lesson.

Going inward with Mozart!

September 15, 2016

Sometimes, we are at odds with the entire world around us. At this point, people turn inward and start asking questions, like: "where did I go wrong?" After all the humdrums of the mind stops, we often start looking for inspiration on how to start fresh in a new quality? At this point, we don’t just want specific answers, but, a new way of life. Teenagers, in the making of themselves as adults, go through this on a regular basis. Nature, music, character trends, they grab and size it on, then toss and try on something else… but, where to find a real anchor in life?

I've heard a cultural historian describe Mozart: frail, small in stature and presence, coming off as physically weak and fragile, but, in spite of all the trouble and pain his physical weakness have caused, the one benefit or point of anchor that can challenge the soul non-stop is the conviction which he lived with that each and every day may be his very last. Hence, as the historian convincingly states, each such a day in Mozart's life had to have a finale worthy of Mozart… nothing could be left for tomorrow. Perhaps, this is the mindset of the immortals: emptying yourself out for the best possible creation of the current moment.

So, what do you get when you combine inner silence and Mozart's legacy? "Awake, dear heart, awake!"
